# Advice for 20 year olds - Alex Hormozi

## -Take asymmetric bets
### take more bets / take more risky bets
you have everything to gain and nothing to lose. Take as many shots as possible because downside = 0. Only time in career it's possible is when you are young. Don't be stopped by others opinions.
Every failure = experience, meaning you will never start from scratch again. You either win or fail and learn. Success is the stacking of little things.
Success bubbles underneath the surface, than all at once explodes, there is no such thing as "overnight success".
You can fail an infinite amount of time. You only truly fail when you stop playing the game. The point is to continue on. You can play the game as long as you don't quit.
Lack of experience needs to be made up by effort & hours. Effort and hours overtime = experience. How much experience gained in a time span is determined by how much effort and hours.
## - Don't follow your passion
### competence = passion
passion comes from competence. We usually like the things we are good at. Becoming good is boring and hard. Playing the game and getting good at the game are different animals. Your passions change as you age, meaning that if you follow your passions forever, you will never settle down long enough to become the best.
Look for things you are good at that people spend money on. Now, get better at it. Thats your ticket.

## - Get good at one thing
### shine a beam of attention on one thing
Focus on one thing, stop dividing your attention. A ray of light that is divided will never be as powerful as a ray magnified onto one point.
A generalists will always be beat by a specialist. Pick one thing and go all in. Become the one who is hard to beat.

Its hard to focus on one thing. How skilled you are = how valuable you are.

If the facts about you are not compelling, change the facts. Become a new truth.
You will be rewarded for determination, determination > intelligence

reminds me of [[deliberate practice]].
## - Stop networking
### focus on the work, networking is a secondary
beginning is the exploration phase, than the exploitation phase comes later. Exploration phase involves finding what you are good at, what you are naturally better at. Then exploitation is the cutting of fat and hard focusing on your specialization. Taste then exploit.

Work > networking. Benefits from working far outweigh the networking. Failing work = more harm then not networking. If work = success, then networking doors will remain open and 100 more will present themselves

Losers congregate, winners isolate. Not everyone is going to win. When you look around and see that you are different than others, thats good. If those people don't live the life you want then why would you live like them? 

Raise the people you surround yourself with = raise life

Work ethic is the universal currency of respect. Hustle. People are happy to help if they feel like you will act on it.

## - Money loves speed
### execution > planning
When you have all the information, the opportunity is most likely gone. Time spent deciding should never be > execution time + fix time(in event of failure).

Uncertainty is the job. You have to comfortable in ambiguity. Decide and act quick.

So few decisions are irreversible. Time spent waiting for decision = time spent not acting. Planning is always easier than execution, execute. Decide now. Never in your vocabulary should you put any decision until the end of the week, the sooner the better. Trying and failing = faster learning than deliberating. At most, decide by the end of the hour.

## - Pay off your debt
### minimize what you don't know but should at all cost
The biggest debt is [[knowledge debt]], the cost of what you don't know but should. The knowledge that pushes you toward you goal faster. Can never be paid off if you don't learn. Learning stems from failure. Be willing to lose money, time, judgements from others, hits to the ego, all in exchange to learn.
You have to learn how to lose before you can learn how to win.

## - Know the price of inaction
### Inaction is more expensive than action
If you believe you are going to be doing something sooner or later, wouldn't you rather do it sooner? You can begin to reap what you sowed so much sooner.
Inaction stems from avoiding the cost of potential failure. Are you worried about failure because you are worried what someone is going to say to you? Name that voice, who is it? Doesn't it sound crazy when you realize?

## - Get over yourself
### Focus on who you are / can be, not what you were
lamenting your circumstances does nothing for you. The vast majority would rather protect their ego, blame the external, then complain.
Losers define themselves by what has happened them, the past. Winners define themselves by what they are capable of, where they are headed, the future.

## - Solver bigger problems
### Big problems = big money
To earn X amount of money, you have to solve problems worth X amount of money. To earn X amount of money, you have to go through the pain worth X amount of money. 

Not knowing anything is a constant. You will never solve it. 

Big goals and small goals are usually just about as hard. If its going to be hard either way, you might as well think big.

Competition over big goals actually = less competition.

Learn to be obsessive. To be extraordinary, you have to be extraordinary. 

## - Obsession is the price of entry
### consistent effort is the bare minimum
[[obsession = consistent effort]]. Most people will go through their lives never truly trying.

Typical hobbies are for typical people. Don't worry about doing what typically considered fun.

all judgements and insults can be distilled to "you do not live your life in a way I prefer". 

Obsessed people don't think themselves obsessed. They see everyone else as plain, surface level. To be the best you can't worry about work-life balance. To be a "workaholic" is a given.

## - Work hard and smart
### You can't outcompete smart people with smarts
 You can't "work smarter not harder" your way to being the top. After a certain point, you will begin to compete with people who are also smart. So you have to outpace their energy. You have to out hustle them.

## - Accept boredom
### the path to the top is lonely, boring, and painful
To achieve something you have to accept boredom or pain. The bigger the goal the more of both. Consistency never looks impressive in the moment, only in the end.
It's very hard to lose if you show up everyday. ([[The Law of Accumulation]])

## - Accept sacrifice
### What are you willing to give up in pursuit of your goal?
What are the things you currently have that you are willing to trade for what you want?
over lived / under skilled 30 year old or under lived / over skilled 30 year old? Your choice, what do you want to be?
[[All You Have In Life is Time and Energy|Everything you spend your time on rather than working on your goal, you are deeming more important than your goal.]]
In a year, are you even going to remember what you are wasting your time on? We have plenty of time, we just waste the time we have.

## - Final Remarks, key takeaway
### Alex Hormozi's advice distilled to a sentence
Figure out what you want, ignore the opinions of others, do so much volume it would be unreasonable to not be successful.
