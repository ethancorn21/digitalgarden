[[macroeconomics]]



narrowest definition of money = m1 money. High liquidity. Assets != money because low liquidity

Interest rate on Y axis, Money supply on X axis. Demand for money downward sloping. If the interest rate is high, the demand for money is high, but the supply of money is low. As the demand for money drops, the interest rate drops, and the supply of money increase.

Transaction demand and asset demand - they want to buy consumer goods and buy assets

If the interest rate is high the opportunity cost of holding money is high, you do not want to hold a lot of liquid cash.

The interest rate = the point on the graph where the money supply meets the demand of money. Money supply = vertical

expansionary monetary policy = increase supply of money = decrease interest rates = increase investments = increase aggregate demand

contractionary monetary policy = decrease supply of money = increase interest rates = decrease investment spending = decrease aggregate demand

Shifters of money demand
1) price level
2) incomes
3) technology

3 shifter of money supply
1) reserve requirement
2) discount rate - the feds interest rate basically
3) open market operations - when the fed buys or sells bonds (securities, t bills) to banks.

fed buys bonds = money supply increase
fed sells bonds = money supply decrease

this is because government gets the money meaning available money supply is decreased as money is taken out of the system
