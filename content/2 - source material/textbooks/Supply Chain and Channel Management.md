[[marketing]] [[school]]

marketing channel management = supply chain management
- direct channel - manufacturer to consumer
- indirect channel - manufacturer to middleman (or middlemen) then customer.

manufacturers -> wholesalers -> retailers

distribution center = facility where goods are received, stored, and shipped to company stores
- sales teams need to coordinate with distribution centers to ensure the sales they make can be fulfilled

fulfillment center vs distribution center:
- fulfillment centers ship directly to customers
- distribution centers ship directly to retailers

relationships are about meeting each others needs. 

conventional marketing channel - each member pursues its own goals, with little respect paid towards other channel members.

types of power:
- reward 
- coercive
- referent
- expertise
- information
- legitimate

UPC code = black and white barcodes

information flows in the marketing channels:
- customer to stores
- store to buyers
- buyer to manufacturers
- store to manufacturers
- store to distribution centers
- manufacturer to distribution center and buyer

mobile task management - wireless network, receives demand notification and enables quick responses.

Remember, **wholesalers are businesses that sell to other businesses**
