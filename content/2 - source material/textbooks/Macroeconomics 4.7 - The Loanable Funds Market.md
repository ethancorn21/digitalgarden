the loanable funds make up how much money is there to be loaned

Loanable funds market brings together borrows and lenders

low interest rate = increased demand
high interest rate = decreased demand

Low interest rate = low savings rate
high interest rate = high savings rate

On loanable funds market graph, supply = savers (banks get their loanable funds from people who deposit money into their bank). Demand = borrowers.

low interest rate = low amount of savers = low supply, but also means a lot of borrowers.

the supply curve of the graph (the savers) also includes government saving, what's left over after all government expenditure. If government spending increases, supply shrinks (or demand increases).
