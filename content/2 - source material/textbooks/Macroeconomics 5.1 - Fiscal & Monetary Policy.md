Economy can do only 3 things:
Recession
Full employment
Inflationary gap

Getting out of recession
- no policy - wages go down = cost go down = aggregate supply goes up.

fiscal policy - consists of Government spending and taxation
- Government spending increases = aggregate demand increases
- Taxes decrease = disposable income increases = consumption increase = aggregate demand increases

monetary policy - controlling the money supply to control interest rates and shift aggregate demand
- in recession, increase money supply = lower interest rates = increased investments = increased aggregate demand
- money supply increases with reserve requirement, discount rate, open market operations (buying and selling bonds)
