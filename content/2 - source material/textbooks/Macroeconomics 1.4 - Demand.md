Law of Demand - inverse relationship between price and quantity demanded
Substitution effect - When price for product goes down or up, people will move toward or away from product

[[income effect]]:
Lower price = will buy more than before
Higher price = will buy less than before

[[Law of diminishing marginal utility]]
 can be thought of as a law of decreasing additional satisfaction
as you consume more of a product the satisfaction decreases with each new consumption.

5 shifters of demand
1) taste and preference
2) number of consumers
3) price of related goods
	- substitutes, compliments
4) income 
	- normal goods
		- Income and demand are directly related
	- inferior goods
		- income and demand are inversely related
5) expectations

Change in quantity demanded vs change in demand
moving along the curve = change in quantity demanded
moving the curve = change in demand

Price changes ONLY the quantity demanded not the demand
ONLY the 5 shifters can change demand
