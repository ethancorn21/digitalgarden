Trade is good, exchanging services is good. Mutually beneficial exchange, both parties benefit.

[[Absolute Advantage]]
Producer can produce more than any other producer given the same amount of resources

[[Comparative Advantage]]
Producer can produce a thing at a lower opportunity cost than any other producer

Specialization determined by per unit opportunity cost.

Terms of Trade
determined by Comparative advantage and opportunity cost which equal a mutually beneficial trade. Both countries need to trade for things which their opportunity cost is higher than. If opportunity cost to produce 1 plane is 3 cars for Producer A and 1 Plane for 1 Car for Producer A. Then, they would trade for 2 cars for 1 plane.

### Hacks for Comparative Advantage
1) recognize if its a output or input question, is it asking what is going out or coming in?
	 - Output questions - you want the most production, better producer = absolute advantage
	 - Input questions - you want fewer numbers, less: land, resources, labor, entrepreneurship (factors of production) to produce a good. Absolute advantage = lower numbers
2) Per Unit Opportunity Costs
	 - Output questions - O.O.O = Output, Other goes Over
	- Producing X over Y. 1X cost y/x. 1Y cost x/y.
	- Input questions - IOU = Input, Other goes Under
	- Producing X and Y. 1X cost X/Y. 1Y cost Y/X
3) its a 50/50, choose the one with the lower opportunity cost as the comparative advantage
4) Terms of trade
		- any # between cost for the other produced thing for both producers
		- Producer 1 produces 1X for 1/2Y
		- Producer 2 produces 1/4X for 5Y
		- anything between 1X and 1/4X is good
		- anything between 1/2Y and 5 Y is good
5) Quick Comparative advantage
	- For Output questions:
		-cross multiple produced goods
		-higher one has the comparative advantage.![[Screenshot 2025-10-16 at 9.53.14 PM.png]]
		- US has comparative advantage in cars, China in planes
	- For Input Questions
		- Flip it around, lower number wins![[Screenshot 2025-10-16 at 9.54.49 PM.png]]
