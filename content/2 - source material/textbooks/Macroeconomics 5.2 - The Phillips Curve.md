shows relationship between inflation and unemployment
inflation on Y axis, Unemployment on X axis.

Short run Phillips curve = downward sloping. High interest rates = low unemployment rates in the short run (positive output gap). Low interest rates = high unemployment in the short run negative output gap). The middle between positive and negative output gap = full employment.

Long run Phillips curve = straight line up and down. Means in the long run there is not relationship between inflation and unemployment. 

whenever the economy adjust in the long run, always end back up at full employment.

Anytime there is an increase or decrease in aggregate demand, it will move along the Phillips Curve. An increase in aggregate demand = lower unemployment rate = higher interest rates. So the movement on the Phillips curve when there is an increase in aggregate demand is is to the left.

When aggregate demand decreases = increased unemployment rate = less consumption = lower interest rates. So the Phillips curve when there is a decrease in aggregate demand will move to the right.

An increase or decrease in Aggregate supply *SHIFTS* the short run Phillips curve to the left or right.
decreased supply = higher unemployment = shifts right (anything that is expected to increase inflation at a given unemployment rate)
increased supply = lower unemployment = shift left (anything that is expected to decrease inflation at a given unemployment rate)
