nominal v.s real gdp

nominal - not adjusted
real - adjusted for inflation from a base year

GDP deflator - deflates nominal GDP to get real GDP
(nominal GDP / Real GDP ) * 100
Lets see formula pops out 125. This means prices have increased 25% since the base year

### practice

1) nominal GDP = 100B, Real GDP = 80$, Deflator = (100/80) * 100 = *125* = 25% increase from base year
2) Real GDP = 200, Deflator = 120, nominal GDP = (X/200) * 100  = 120
- 120/ 100 = 1.2 | X / 200 = 1.2. | 200 * 1.2 = 240 | *Nominal GDP = 240*
1) Nominal = 300, Deflator = 150, Real GDP = (300/X) * 100 = 150
- 150 / 100 = 1.5 | 1.5X = 300 | 300/1.5 = 200 | *Real GDP = 200*
