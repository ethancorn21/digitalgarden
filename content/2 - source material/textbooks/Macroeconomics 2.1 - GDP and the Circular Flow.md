[[macroeconomics]]

GDP (Gross Domestic Product) - value of everything a country produces within a year.
Only end goods count. What the final consumer buys.

### 3 ways to calculate GDP:

##### Expenditures approach - focused on purchases by consumers (consumers spending), businesses (investment spending), Government (government spending), net exports (imports - exports).
GDP = C + I + G + (X-M)

##### income approach - National income = wages + rent + interest + profit

##### Value added approach - adds value at each stage of production process

### Circular Flow Model:
Split into 2 sections Private and Public sector
the private sector consists of businesses and households. Households *supply* resources to the resource market. Businesses demand *resources* from the resource market and *supply* goods and services to the goods and services market. Then households *demand* goods and services from the goods and services market. 
When flipped, households spend *$* in the goods and services market, businesses receive *revenue* from the goods and services market, pay *cost* to the resources market which gives *income* to households.

Public sector includes government spending and receiving
government buys things called *government spending* and produces thing called *publics goods*
government takes money from households and businesses in the form of *tax*. But also gives it back to businesses and households in the form of *subsidies* and *welfare*.
