Output gap?

The Long Run Aggregate Supply Curve is vertical at the max potential output when no unemployment exist but the natural unemployment rate.

If the Aggregate demand (the total demand at a price level(avg cost of all goods)) increases. The price level will increase, which mean producers will want to get some of that money so they will produce more. So, in the short run GDP and Price level increase. Unemployment drops below the natural unemployment and output rises above potential output (*inflationary gap*). In the Long run, wages will rise because of increased prices = resources more expensive = cost of production higher = less aggregate supply in the long run. So in the Long run, the price level is now permanently higher, GDP returns back to potential output, and unemployment rate returns back to natural rate.

Negative Output gap = current output is less than potential output
Positive output gap = current output is greater than potential output
means resources are over or underutilized. (specifically unemployment)

In recessionary gap, unemployment rises about the natural unemployment rate, which means there is increased competition of workers for a smaller pool of jobs. A lot of potential workers = lower wages = lower cost of production = more supply = cheaper goods. In the long run, unemployment will fall = higher wages = higher resource cost = output returns back to potential.
