[[macroeconomics]]


Banks make money by lending money. Can't lend all of it's money.
Required Reserve = how much total deposits the banks must hold and not loan out.

Each loan will make its way back to a bank eventually which will loan it out again, saving 10%, process repeats.
$100 deposit, 90 loaned 10 saved
$90 deposit, 81 loaned 9 saved.
Eventually cycle repeats, and $900 is created out of initial deposit of $100.
Money multiplier = 1 / reserve ratio (yes, same as MPC and MPS with government spending)
if banks required to save 10% then, 1/.1 = 10 money multiplier. 10 * money loaned out = how much is then created from initial deposit. From the above example 10 * 90 = 900.
