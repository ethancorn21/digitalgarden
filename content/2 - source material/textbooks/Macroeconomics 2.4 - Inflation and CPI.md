[[macroeconomics]]


Measuring inflation - look at specific goods and track changes over time
Inflation rate - percent change in price over time ((CPI / previous CPI) * 100)
Price Indices - number assigned to year to show how price changed compared to base year
- usually 1982-84
- Base Year = 100 CPI, if CPI = 250, prices have risen 150%

CPI = (Price of market basket / price of market in base year) * 100

### Practice - Base Year = 2000 ($50)

1999 - $40  : (40 / 50) * 100 = 80 CPI
2000 - $50 : (50/50) * 100 = 100 CPI
2001 - $55 : (55/50) * 100 = 110 CPI
2002 - $60 : (60/50) * 100 = 120 CPI 
2003 - $75 : (75/50) * 100 = 150 CPI
2004 - $100 : (100/50) * 100 = 200 CPI
2005 - $200 : (200/50) * 100 = 400 CPI
