Short run and long run aggregate supply

short run - as price increased quantity, suppliers are incentivized to increase supply and vice versa.
changed by anything that changes how much of a good is produced
negative and positive supply shock
change in expected price level shifts the supply BUT NOT A CHANGE IN PRICE

long run aggregate supply curve - shows how price of wages and resources adjust when there is a change in price level

### example
1) decrease aggregate supply : less workers = less productivity
2) increase aggregate supply: more machines = more productivity
3) decrease aggregate supply: machines stop working = less productivity
4) increase aggregate supply: more money = more everything = more productivity
5) decrease aggregate supply : less buying power = less productivity
