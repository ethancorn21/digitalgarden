[[macroeconomics]]
[[spending multiplier]]

Spending multiplier - The government spends money, that money is someone's income. Only 2 things you can do with income : spend and save. That person saves a portion of that income and then spends the rest. That expenditure becomes someone else's income and they save a little and spend the rest. The cycle keeps repeating. Think of trickle down economics when you think of spending multiplier
[[Marginal Propensity to Consume]] - how much of the income they will spend
[[Marginal Propensity to Save]]  - how much of the income they will save

both should add up to equal 1, again there is only 2 things you can do with income.

simple spending multiplier formula = 1 / MPS (marginal propensity to save)

if MPC is .5 and MPS = .5 : then 1/ .5 = 2 : so if the government spend $2 billion, $4 billion would be spent by the economy.

EX: MPC = .9 : gov wants total spending to increase $20 billion. Multiplier = 10 ; $2 billion for government to spend to reach this goal


tax multiplier = 1 less than the spending multiplier ^ instead of 10, it would be 9
