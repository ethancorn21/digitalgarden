# Montesquieu on Comparative Happiness
 >"If we only wanted to be happy, it would be easy; but we want to be happier than other people, which is almost always difficult, since we think them happier than they are." - Charles de Montesquieu
 
 Comparative happiness is destined to fail. As you well know by now the brain is dog shit at estimating anything. And yet, we rely on these dog shit estimation of other's happiness to measure our on by. We over attribute happiness to others, as such our own happiness is bound to become quite minuscule in comparison.
 This is what kills me about FOMO. It is so irrational to think your life would be marginally better if you were doing anything other than what you are doing now. It speaks to the placing of your self identity and happiness into the hands of others.
 
 Comparative happiness will only ever get worse as social media usage deepens. We are beginning to see the downside of integrating social media into the social sphere, or perhaps it's replacement. As the social structure of society becomes increasingly reliant on taking the post as a fact of life, so to will the projected happiness in that post become a perceived fact of life. A fact we can't bare to live up to. The social ramifications in the mind of humans because of a plague of comparative happiness the likes of which we have not seen will quite probably lead us into untouched levels of degeneracy and unhappiness that will be hidden and there will be a brighter, better world that will be projected over it.
