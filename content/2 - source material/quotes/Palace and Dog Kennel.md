# Palace and Dog Kennel

> “A thinker erects an immense building, a system, a system which embraces the whole of existence and world-history, etc. And if we contemplate his personal life, we discover to our astonishment this terrible and ludicrous fact, that he himself personally does not live in this immense high-vaulted palace, but in a barn alongside of it, or in a dog kennel, or at the most in the porter’s lodge. If one were to take the liberty of calling his attention to this by a single word, he would be offended. For he has no fear of being under a delusion, if only he can get the system completed… by means of the delusion.” - Soren Kierkegaard, The Sickness Unto Death

Kierkegaard's humor is something to truly behold. At it's most basic, take the smartest guy you know and think about how he lives. Does he embody that knowledge or big brain of his? Probably not. Now, look at the lives of philosophers, psychologist, and all these other "greats". Look at their lives, its often times the exact opposite of the views and ideals they try so hard to create and uphold. All philosophers, scientists, and great thinkers die knowing just as much about the meaning of life and purpose of our being as the person who hasn't thought once. The delusion of creating something grander than our circumstance, than our reality, is enough for most everyone to be ok with living in the potential rather than the actual.

As an example take Nietzsche. The man who thought of the Will to Power never encountered much power in his lifetime beyond the realm of his mental processes. A heart broken man with more failed relationships than money in his pocket at the time of his death. Despite valuing the pre-christian values of power, striving, and domination, he never attained much of it.
