Paid in Capital
- capital stocks - divided into shares

- Outstanding shares of stock - # of stock in shareholder hands

### par value, stated value, no-par value

par value is the stated value of a stock by the charter. Companies must keep par value * issued stock in their accounts, this is called legal capital or stated capital.
- but when a company has a stated value of $10 and it sells a share for $20, the common-stock account would be credit (common stock is an equity) $10, and the extra $10 would be credited to the Additional Paid-In Capital account. Then debit cash for total amount

Classification of common stock (find better definitions)
- common stock
- preferred stock
	- paid dividend before common stock holders, payment preference as well in liquidation

A charter application is signed by the prospective stockholders called incorporators or promoters, then it is given to the state for approval

stock dividend : cash dividend but with stock instead of cash.
- small stock dividend - 25% of less of outstanding shares
- large stock dividend - >25% of outstanding shares

how to calculate dividend payout:
1. calculate preferred dividend requirement
	- Shares * par value * dividend rate = total preferred dividend
2. subtract preferred dividend from total dividend "pot"
3. If preferred stock is cumulative, you must pay those "debts" off first before common shareholders get paid

treasury stock is a *contra-equity account*, it reduces total stock holder equity on the balance sheet
