### Jiang Xueqin Civilization Lectures
### summary:
Hunter gatherers would go to religious sites and meet up with shamans as well as other hunter gatherer groups. Shamans would create these religions and attract hunter gatherers. As some hunter gatherers decided to follow shamans, they settled down at religious sites. While still being hunter gatherers they relied on the local wildlife, which would be depleted over time. So, they had to resort to agriculture to sustain themselves while staying at their religious site. Agriculture at the time was not the main source of food, as seen at Jericho, but rather like a garden. If you wanted to do agriculture you would. As they farmed more and for longer, to sustain growing populations, the land would become arid and they would then need to relocate. But religion comes along as well, meaning that agriculture does too. So, transition to agriculture begins (over thousands of years).

### Traditional story of agriculture:
We were hunter gatherers roaming in bands, food not guaranteed
discovery of agriculture = settling down, surplus of food
surplus of food = politics, religion, arts, writing
the pivotal step toward modernity stems from agriculture
BUT, no evidence for it.

"We did not domesticate wheat, wheat domesticated us", think about it, wheat had to do a lot of work to survive without humans. Now, humans do all the work and wheat thrives.

Agriculture terrible for nutrition. Hunter gatherers bigger and stronger than farmers. Farming way more dangerous than farming. Farms dirty, dangerous livestock, civilization = unclean = disease.  
### Theories of why we transitioned to agriculture:
1) coercion, strong people didn't want to work so forced others to.
	- But, humans can just run away, kill the strong, team up, etc.
2) War, defense easier when in one place
	- But, no large scale violence among different groups, but a lot of in group violence, don't really find a lot of weapons.
3) Respect for elders, every culture has at least some respect for the elderly
	- But, for much of history, the world was a cycle. Death comes after and before birth. So, death not really a big deal. Evidenced by religion
4) Religion, we settled down in order to practice a religion
### Gobekli Tepe:
Religious site, old as hell
Place of religious worship. Religious site oldest, newer houses added later.
Hunter gatherers met at religious spots. Religious centers also acted as meeting spots for mates. Religious spots ran by shamans. Possible that Shamans became like celebrities, hunter gatherers come to meet them and others. Over time some people chose to stay with them, settled at religious spots, built houses. After their death, they continue to celebrate them.
Structure has cosmological significance. Their intention was to connect with an 'older world'. Their science was their religion.
Humans, animals, trees all seen as equal back then. Pillars of Gobekli Tepe seen as asking for forgiveness, to channel their hunting power, to make friends with the predators. Religion celebrating the animals and trying to channel their power.

### Jericho:
Built by hunter gatherers who were sedentary. Meaning they moved around, but still stayed in the same area. They had domesticated crops, but chose to not do agriculture at scale.
Cult of the Skull - worshipped ancestors. Took skulls, kept in houses, worshipped it. [[ancestor worship]].  Dead ancestor = connection to spirit world. Their method of scientific discovery. Their understanding of the world, beliefs, intelligence just as sophisticated as us today.
Tower of Jericho - religious structure, that covered the village in darkness at specific time of the year. Making village dark = collapsing space between village and space, 'the outer world'. By making village dark it was like magic, if can perform magic, religion = correct.

### Catal Huyuk: 
One of the first religious communities. Before, hunter gathers religion was somewhere you went. Now, religion is with you 24/7. Everything now explained by religion.
Bird painting in every house. Theorized to represent the mother goddess. When someone dies, sky burial, let birds eat bodies then take bones and bury it in your house. 
Complete religion very attractive to people, remember people don't like not knowing something, ambiguity.
Bull painting w/ worshippers. Bull as an opposite of mother goddess figure. Masculine, vitality, energy. Only when bull and mother goddess come together does life occur.
