### Jiang Xueqin Civilization Lectures

### summary:
For most of human history humans were peaceful. Animism promotes peace and togetherness with nature.

---

Humans for most of our history were peaceful, egalitarian and artistic. 
We weren't non-violent, but violence was contained to our group, it was not organized battles. 
We were very equal. In excavations of sites, we see that all houses are about of equal size. But in burial, there are some that are more lavish than others = higher status. 
Art was the expression and celebration of their religion.

Understanding religion allows us to understand the mindset of early humans.

Every society has a creation myth. Every creation myth explains the legal system, the rules of a society, what you can and can't do. 


Religion has to be believed to be just as correct as science, this is accomplished in 3 ways:
1) **grandness**
2) **completeness**, everything you see in the world has to be explained
3) **unity**, a beginning and an end

Religion centers the life of modern hunter gatherers. Everything they do has to have a religious significance. Ritual is the expression of religion in everyday practice. Behind the ritual there has to be a belief system. The rituals have meaning and purpose.
Modern day hunter gatherers, believe in animism (unity of life). Before you hunt you must pay tribute to the animals. A life taken without permission will be revenged by spirit of nature in the form of death. Gives us glimpse into how ancient hunter gatherers thought. 
In modern hunter gatherer religions, before hunts shaman 'transform' into animals to celebrate the animals and ask for permission to partake in the hunt. In ancient times, we see cave paintings of shamans dressed as animals *doing the exact same thing*!  Job of humans = to help maintain the balance and harmony of nature.

Modern mind = man v. nature
premodern mind = man is apart of nature

modern man afraid of the unknown because we want control. Premodern mind trust nature, they believe nature knows them and they know nature.
