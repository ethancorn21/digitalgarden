# life long learner
A philosophy that entails a commitment and dedication to continually expanding your professional and personal knowledge / skills over the course of your lifetime. Characterized by a deep curiosity and hunger to learn / apply new knowledge to ones life.

For me, I have made the commitment to be a lifelong learner while I am young. The field I wish to get into (technology) is constantly evolving, as such those that do not learn and adapt get let behind. While yes, continual professional knowledge is a great, to me its a given. You are expected to do it so that you can keep progressing towards mastery. 

What is not a given, is continual personal learning. Many people equate learning with school. As such a hatred for school and formal education becomes a hatred for learning. It should be important to instill the idea that learning does not automatically mean schooling. If you adopt this mindset I hope you like where you are, you ain't going anywhere else.

Anyone can read some books every now and then and call themselves a lifelong learner. If you wish for your journey to be of any consequence, you need to be able to intake information, store it for later use, and then be able to recall it when necessary. Do yourself a favor and adopt a mindset of malleability. The conditions of survival rarely change. But the conditions to thrive are as ever fleeting and ever changing as the seasons. Do yourself a favor and [[learning |learn to learn]].
