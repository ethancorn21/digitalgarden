# Capillary Action in Trees

Trees move water up their stem through water sticking to cellulose, and then that water pulling up more water with it. At the top of the tree, water evaporates off of the vast amount of leaves in the trees canopy. This evaporative effect produces so much energy that it is able to pull water hundreds of feet in the air. 

If you know anything about water limitations in physics, you will know that we do not have the technical capacity to pull water through a pipe that is 10 meters tall. Crazy to think about and not a limitation you hear about everyday. 10 meters. This is because the energy exerted over the water at 10 meters is so great that it boils the water instantly. 

Yet, trees like the California Redwood are able to draw water hundreds of feet into the air. Precisely evolved organisms.
