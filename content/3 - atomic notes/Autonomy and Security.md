# Autonomy and Security

we have a habit of giving up autonomy for perceived security.

We gave up a belief in ourselves, a responsibility for our actions, that what ever was to come was by our own making. Then, we gave that belief, that responsibility for our actions, to a higher power.
Where / when in religious history do we say "i did it *for* God" versus "God made me do it".

In the modern day, we allow ourselves to live in a surveillance state with government mandates that erode our rights as people. Why? Obviously when you spell it out this way of course it is awful. But by framing each ingress into our rights as "needed for security" it activates a primal condition in us. Security = safety, and of course we want safety. We became social creature operating in groups because it affords more safety. We are safety oriented creatures. And so, when tangible safety is offered in exchange for abstract freedom. That's a trade that is hard to fathom as such, we go along with it. Thus, we exchange autonomy for security. It only takes one to look at the Patriot Act, formation of the NSA, formation of religion, the rise of the CCTV to see this obvious trend. Each time, we want security so we give up our autonomy over our selves.
