# The Science of Charisma

Charisma is not so much about being attractive as people think. In fact, quantitively is makes up only about ~10.5% of what makes someone charismatic. The four things that actually make up the majority of charisma are: communicative, attracts admiration and inspires, honest and reliable. These thing can be achieved by:

1) having a vision
2) constantly working toward that vision
3) being authentic
4) being compassionately honest
5) speaking clearly

You see how these build off of each other? By having a vision, you think about what you must do to work towards it. By constantly working toward a vision, you become naturally authentic because you are acting in accordance with your values and beliefs. When you have a vision to work towards, the anxiety of ambiguity is not there anymore. It allows you to be more honest with yourself and others, because your goal is to be working towards that vision. Not pleasing yourself or others. And so you can begin to say what you really mean to say (tempered with compassion of course), which feeds back into being an authentic person. Speaking clearly helps you communicate your vision with yourself and others, be more authentic because you can accurately describe what you wish to say, and be more compassionate because you can approach difficult things with a precise way of speaking. 

So then, whats left for you to do is to firstly decide on your vision. How do you want to live your life? What can you imagine the you on your deathbed looking back and saying, "i'm glad I chose that life". Whatever it is. Be relentless in your pursuit of it. Ensure that it is something that helps others as well.
