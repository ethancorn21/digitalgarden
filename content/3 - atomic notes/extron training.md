# Extron training

digital vs analog
- analog = sound waves carried over cable
- digital = sound converted to bytes to be send over cable

digital matrix processor (DMP) turns audio signal into acceptable form to be sent over a network (usually w/ Dante protocol).

Mic - preamp - amp - output

preamp takes the low energy signals from the mic and increases them enough for further processing down the line
- sound boards (aka mixers) can also be added between the preamp and amp

there is an acceptable range of sound. Anything above is distortion, anything below is noise.

Digital Signal Processors (DSP) are used to mix, optimize, and fine-tuning signals.
- put between pre amp and amp
- can automatically dim output in favor of other outputs (called ducking)

amplifiers take a low level signal and increase the energy enough to drive a big speaker. Think of car audio. Everyone has aftermarket amps to power their subs.
