# The Freudian Aggressive Oral Phase

The oral phase is the first stage of Freud's theory of psychosexual development. Fixation here is caused by over or under indulgence of breastfeeding as a baby. This can lead to fixation as an adult. Fixation will often manifest itself in two ways, oral aggressive and oral passive.

Both are characterized by nail biting, cigarette smoking, chewing. But the difference is theorized to mainly come out in personality, ie. cynical vs overly optimistic.
