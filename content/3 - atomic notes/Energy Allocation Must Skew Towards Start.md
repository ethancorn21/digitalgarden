# Energy Injected Into Building Lifecycle

> energy put in early in the process pays off tenfold and energy put in at the end of the program pays off negative tenfold
> - Ben Horowitz, in High Output Management's foreword.

Can you imagine trying to build the foundations of a great life at 50 versus at 25? Imagine trying to save for retirement at 50 versus 25? Then think how hard things are to change in production versus in design.

A large portion of the total energy and effort that is invested into a project needs to be placed at the beginning. This ensures that the entirety of the project is not built on shaky foundations. How much harder it is to repair a foundation than a roof?

This not only applies to work, but also any undertaking that you will find yourself on. "Measure twice and cut once" is an adage for a reason.
