# knowledge debt

The time, money, energy, and / or ego cost of what you don't currently know but should. 
The idea of not knowing what you don't know correlates heavily with this.

The goal should be to always minimize what you don't know but should by any means necessary.
### example: 
Take the history of manufacturing as an example. For a long time, humans were assembling things by having the product sit on the factory floor and each worker would build each product and walk it to where it needed to go. Their knowledge debt at the time was that there are far more efficient ways in which they could manufacture, like the belt, specialized tasks, efficiency of motion, etc.

Another example, when you walk through the world with your current lens of how you see it. There are billions and trillions of different ways to see the world, who is to say that they way you currently see it is the current way to go about it, as such reading books and doing anything you can to see through new a new lens minimizes your knowledge debt by allowing you more perspectives with which to see the world.
