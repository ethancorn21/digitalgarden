# Free Will Thought Experiment

if free will is a higher order function above reactivity, but still draws from past experiences to make decisions. What happens if there are no past experiences? What happens to supposed free will then?

What if we took a being and this being only had the ability to be present in the future. It has no memory of the past. Obviously it would be confused but that's beside the point. (*edit 1.22.26* would it be confused? I don't think so. The being wouldn't know one moment from the next)

Faced with a decision, how would this being begin to make that decision? It could not draw on past experiences. It could not communicate to gather more information. It seems its decision is locked into place by its present circumstance. 

When the being comes to a decision, can that decision really be regarded as free will? Their decision is based more on chance and psychological affinity for a choice rather than consciousness free will reasoning.

You might say that because they chose, they were exercising their free will, but I disagree. A decision implies their was consideration from the participant. Is the being even able to give consideration to it's option if it has no past experience?
