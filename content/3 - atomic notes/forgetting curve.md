# forgetting curve
The rate at which we forget newly learned information. When information is first learned, that information is forgotten with hours / days unless it is recalled back to working memory. The act of recalling the memory resets the forgetting curve and strengthens the ability of your brain to recall that memory more reliably over longer periods in the future.

Example: Using flashcards to recall facts you learned in class a few hours after learning them. These facts will not be forgotten for a day or two, at which point you will then recall them again, and reset the forgetting curve. The process repeats until your forgetting curve is over the span of months / years.
