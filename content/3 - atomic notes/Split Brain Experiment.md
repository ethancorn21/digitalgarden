# Split Brain Experiment

There was a study that gave instructions to people whose brain hemisphere was isolated because their corpus callosum, the part that connects the two brain hemispheres, was severed. One side of the brain knew the instructions and did as they were told. The other half did not have the memory of the instruction. Yet, when asked why they just did what they just did, the subjects created reason for doing it other than that which they were just told. For example, if they were told to walk around and were asked why they did it, they would say "oh i just needed to move around a bit". So there is a part of the brain that interprets and assigns meaning to what it does not know or understand.
