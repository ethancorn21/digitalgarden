# Socrates Fear of Writing and Speaking As a Way To Flesh Out Ideas

Socrates was afraid of the effect that writing would have on the Human thinking ability. With writing, no longer do you have to wholly internalize ideas so that you can reactively and reflexively defend and counter attack ideas. You are able to give off some semblance that you know what you are talking about. 

It is just as important to speak then as it is to write. A good communicator will be able to effortlessly flow between either medium and still convey what they meant.
