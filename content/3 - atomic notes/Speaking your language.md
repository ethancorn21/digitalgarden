# Speaking your language

I am reminded of the story of 2 people talking. One of the guys, a non native English speaker is being made fun of by the other guy, a native English speaker, for his accent. The non native English speaker says to the guy, "I speak English because its the only language *you* know".

How many times have I considered someone unknowledgeable about something because they didn't talk to the proficiency I was used to? How many times were these people speaking the language because its the only language I know?
I know I do this all the time. I dumb things down or don't talk about extremely niche topics, because I want to speak to people in a language they know. If I do it, I know others do as well. 

So, the question is, how can I draw out people to speak in the language they *prefer*? Not the everyday ready man that so many people portray, but the real them. With the real bits of what they wish others understood or want others to know.
