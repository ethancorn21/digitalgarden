# New Experiences Make Life Feel Longer

The reason childhood feels long is because every experience is in someway new. Even the mundane is a flush of new thoughts and new depth to the emotional and intellectual experience. 
But as we grow older, we begin to know what these experiences will contain. We know subconsciously the fixed archetypes of people that we will meet, how we deal with them, if we like them. We know what they will say to our questions and statements. 

Even new locations we can't help but compare to previous places that we have been. Every new experience becomes a comparison to something in the past. We can't help it. The brain is a pattern recognition machine. 

But in order to escape the "life is too short trap", one must find a way to return to that child like state. Every new experience does not become a comparison to what is know, but an exploration into what is not.
