# Consciousness as a Narrative Interface

Recent research has found that we make decisions before our brains can process those decisions. And yet, how often do we think that the self we know came to the conclusion? We begin to backfill in reasoning and logic to fit the decision, not the other way around. 

In patients who have had their brain split, one brain hemisphere was given instructions to follow, when the subject followed the instructions, say getting up from their chair and walking around in a circle. The subject was asked why they had done that, and the subject replied something along the lines of, "oh i just need to walk around". So we can see that even though the brain did not have all the information it need from both sides of the brain, one side of the brain attributed meaning to what is was doing even though it did not know the instructions.

We can abstract this out to brain function and the unconscious. If we are not conscious of our own "instructions", like non-conscious impulses and things of the like, when we move to follow those non-conscious impulses why wouldn't we assign meaning to what we are doing in the same way with the split brain experiment? Think of decisions. If our decisions are mostly non-conscious, why would we not also assign meaning to our decisions?

It seems that Consciousness is a narrative device that assigns meaning so that the viewer, us, has a clue about what we are watching. What does that say about the brains architecture if we leap to conclusions rather than conceptualize the gaps? If our brains are adapted from our environment, what does this say about the environment in which it adapted from?
