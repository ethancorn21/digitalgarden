# curse of knowledge
The more you know about a topic the harder it is to teach to people that don't know about the topic. This is because after years of mental heuristics to mastered knowledge, explaining knowledge in a digestible form often requires the teacher to put themselves into the shoes of the student, but since it has been so long since they were in their shoes, their knowledge of what a beginner knows is skewed toward knowing more than they do. 
Often the best teachers are fellow students that have a solid understanding the concept because they can empathize with what the student knows and is struggling on.

### Example:
Think about how you would teach addition to a child. "easy", I am willing to bet you thought. Would you teach it as a cause and effect? because there is 1 of these things and 2 of these things, together they will form 3? Would you teach finger counting? It hard to imagine just how simple something such as addition would be difficult, but to the child, it is a foreign new concept.
