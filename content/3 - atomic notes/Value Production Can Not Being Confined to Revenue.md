[[value creation]], [[mindful consumption]],[[analysis]], [[Inversion Philosophy]]

Value creation cannot be solely confined to revenue efficiency. There is a certain efficacy from sacrificing something that we want to give users what they need. We see the outset of squeezing pennies out of consumers and we see the pushback from it. Piracy advocates(including me haha) will quote Gabe Newell who said that "Piracy is almost always a service problem". Service problems almost always stem from creating something that is not created to be used, but instead to brag. 

True evolution of expertise is to eventually value simplicity. When you create you must ensure that the function of your creation does not change. Who are the ends users? What do they realistically need/want? It is not about pushing the latest tech or being flashy. It is about creating value and robustness for users.
