# Ben Franklin Effect
People will like someone else more if they help that person.

Which most likely means that people are more likely to help someone they have helped before, not someone who has helped them.

Theorized to be caused by the brain trying to reduce cognitive dissonance.  "if I helped that person it must have been cause I like them". 
Being more unlikely to help someone who has helped you can probably be attributed to perceiving that a favor is due in repayment, so the debtor subconsciously distances themselves from their perceived debt.
