# curiosity

I would much rather be plagued by curiosity like a disease, then randomly stumble upon it as a consequence of divine inspiration. To be subject to the whims of chance that lead many people to ask "why" rather than having an immutable desire for the "why" is to leave the acquisition of new knowledge and experience to chance. Something that just is not quite comfortable to sit with. It is unlikely that I will reach the goals I need to fulfill if i do not allow myself to hone in on every curiosity as a new piece of the knowledge puzzle. More resources to draw from and *apply*.

Are great men plagued with ideas like a disease? ones that they just can't quite rid themselves of? Maybe the key to becoming a master is turn your curiosity into an [[obsession = consistent effort|obsession]]. Something to think about.
