# Social Media Addiction as a Byproduct of Consciousness Projection

We are born with the unique ability to project our consciousness. We somewhat always imagine the location of our consciousness as 2-3 inches behind our forehead. But you can project your consciousness into any area. Imagine yourself in the upper corner of the room you are in now. Watching yourself as if you were a fly on the wall. Amazing how good you are at this isn't it?

Perhaps we developed this ability as a social mechanism. Theory of Mind would have us believe it formed as an ability to empathize and place ourselves into others to see why they do the things they do. But this mechanism didn't stop at being able to do this to other people. Why would it? In our minds, we can never truly understand another person, but we can understand that they have consciousness and thought processes just like the self. They are a black box that we are projecting ideas onto. It's similar to how we project human personalities and desires onto animals. 

Throughout history we have projected consciousness onto myths and stories. Allowing ourselves to live them as if we could actually exists within them. 

In the modern day, social media allows us to project our consciousness into the timeline. Where a new projection comes with a singular swipe. We are able to inhabit a space much the same as our human and fly on the wall projections.
