# Questions for connection
Read This To Understand how to read a book better

[[How To Take Smart Notes]]:
1) How does this fact fit into my idea of...?
2) How can this phenomenon be explained by that theory?
3) are these two ideas contradictory or complementary?
4) Isn't this argument similar to this one?
5) Haven't I heard this before?
6) What does 'x' mean for 'y'?

You want to *reduce [[hindsight bias]] BY ANY MEANS NECESSARY* , this means that anything you read about how to do anything will be poisoned by hindsight bias. The thinking that doing this will get you this. AVOID AT ALL COSTS, IT IS PERVASIVE IN EVERYTHING WE READ.

1) focus on take aways that make your processes better, not "this action results in this"
2) ask yourself, "in what ways could this have gone wrong"
3) ask yourself, "Did they know this would lead to this or is this a conclusion they drew after the fact"
4) Start lookin for things that improve odds not guarantee outcomes
