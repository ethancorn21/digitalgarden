Evolution is not about the survival of the individual. It is about the survival of the genes. Long ago, genes were nothing more than elements. But, over time the atoms that make up elements were able to experience a sort of evolution. Atoms that were able to survive the harsh vacuum moments after the big bang lasted milliseconds longer than others. From this we already see the basis for survival. Soon, atoms gravitated together and through billions of years of chance and physics, these atoms soon started to bind and form together in ways that would form the basis of life. Certain formations stuck together better than others. Certain molecules had a polarity to them, so they could rip apart and "steal" atoms from other molecules.  Emergent Complexity. From these basic roots, comes life. From bacteria to human. This is where we trace our roots back to.  Our skin, our brain, our eyes are forms of those same molecules naturally surviving just with billions more year of emergent complexity. So, our survival is not about the individual self, but survival of the molecules that formed themselves into genes. It's why parents happily sacrifice themselves for their children. Or why we broadly are drawn to family.
