# retrieval practice
The act of recalling learned information actively rather than passively reintaking it. The testing effect can be achieved through flashcards, quizzes, recalling what you just read, etc. By actively recalling the memory, its long term remembrance is strengthened.

### example:
As you read through a book, stop every couple of paragraphs and ask yourself, "what did I just read?". At the end of each chapter, again ask yourself, "what did I just read?". Recall the main ideas, anything you are fuzzy on, go back and clear it up, then test yourself again.
