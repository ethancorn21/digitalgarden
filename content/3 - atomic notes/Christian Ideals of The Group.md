# Christian Ideals of The Group
why do we place such a big emphasis on the Christian ideals of family and community?

From a Nietzschen perspective, the weak come together in herds to appear strong. Much the same as prey animals like ox, sheep, zebras, gazelles, etc all live in packs. Predators though are almost always solitary animals. As the hierarchical structure of the animal kingdom moves towards the apex predator, the size of the group in which each class operates, shrinks. 

But humans have not always been the apex predator. We were on the African Savannas getting mauled by four legged animals and big birds. We had to group together to survive because we were at the middle of the food chain. Not the apex predator and not the lowly prey herbivores. Yet, despite now being undoubtably the top of the top of the food chain in every part of the world, we still cling to this group. Our herd. Our families and our communities. Because yes, it feels good. I believe that it fulfills a "will to survive", a Schopenhauerian view. We often desire a community or place to belong to because evolutionarily, if we didn't have one it would mean death. In our evolutionary tree, [[individualism]] meant death. As the saying goes, "the lone nail that sticks out, gets hammered".
