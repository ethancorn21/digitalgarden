# meetings != productivity

Managers idea of productivity is meetings, because they are managing. Meetings on a calendar are a visible metric of work for managers. 

For individual contributors, those meetings are killers of productivity because they are not contributing but diffusing information like projects statuses and timelines in an inefficient manner. The very nature of IC work states that they must be focused on fixing and implementation. Anything other than these two types of work are distractions.

To increase productivity, ensure that individual contributors are maximally working with no distractions, while managers can stay in meetings. The managers work metric can not be used to measure the ICs work metric. They are fundamentally incompatible.

I think of each team as a LAN. The individual contributors are the end hosts and the managers should be the routers. The managers communicate with other managers and members under those managers through the manager. The members communicate to managers through a service that does not take their time like meetings and can be easily updated with progress checks and information.

A basic fix: Schedule IC meetings in one giant chunk of the day, either in the morning or at the end of the day. This allows the most uninterrupted work, while also allowing information to escape the pits of IC hell. This also allows for increased efficiency of IC time because that 30-45 minutes before a meeting are noncommittal. Meaning that it is hard for IC's to actually work during this time because they are constantly looking at the clock and thinking about the upcoming meeting rather than the work in front of them.
