# deep work
>"deep work: professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit. These efforts create new value, improve your skill, and are hard to replicate." -Cal Newport

I tend to see deep work as an activity itself. One that wraps around the activity of learning or working on something. Deep work is sort of like the barrier that ensures the quality of what you are working on or learning remains high. I characterize my deep work by being in a state of intense concentration, not being distracted, sometimes losing sense of time ([[flow state]]), and heavily interacting / grappling with the materials I am using. 

Do not look at deep work as a state of intense concentration that is kept up for hours and hours, it can be. But most likely it will be short spurts of 30 - 60 minutes of intense concentration followed by a 10 - 15 minute rest.

If you imagine a cartoon, deep work leaves my head smoking after a good session, my mind feels spent. The ability to perform deep work can be trained over time. At first 1 hour in total a day is hard. But masters can achieve 4 hours. The key is to continually push yourself to your limits.
