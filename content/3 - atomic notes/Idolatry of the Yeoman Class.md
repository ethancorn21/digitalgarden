# Idolatry of the Yeoman Class

When we look through the strata of social class, we begin to see a differentiation of high emphasis ideals. Ideals that are seen as the worthiest of ways to see life. Among these: power, money, family, religion / spirituality, social circles, community, pursuit of knowledge, that sort of thing. 

The monk might say higher ideals like spirituality and the pursuit of knowledge are worthy but money is evil. The intelligencia might say a higher ideal like the pursuit of knowledge and the advancement of known human information is a grand way to live. The businessman might say that money and family are his chief goals in life. That he must get money to ensure his families happiness. None more noble than the other I believe.

But why do we as humans place such a high emphasis on the pursuit of these common goals. A means of survival and a means of thriving? or perhaps if we are to think about it in a Nietzschean way, we would say we look towards higher ideals as a way of coping with life. It certainly feels good to tell ourselves that what we are doing has a grander impact beyond the scope of the self. That what we do now is echoing into our external environment, our community, and in turn echoing into eternity.

So what does social class have to do with this? Well, I ask the question : what ideals or actions do different societal levels worship / look up to which play an influencing part in their social class?
