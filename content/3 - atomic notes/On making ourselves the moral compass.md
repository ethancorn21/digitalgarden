# On making ourselves the moral compass

In the absence of a higher power, whether religious or spiritual. The mind becomes the decider of what is moral and right. The problem is that the human moral compass can be reasoned and bargained with. Someone who (consciously or unconsciously) places themselves at the top of the moral control hierarchy, will and can say that they would never do a certain thing. But then, when the reward for doing a certain thing becomes greater than the shame of doing it, the moral compass begins to be reasoned with. The ego begins to shift more towards the id than the superego.

I propose that the vast majority of people, myself included, need to be locked into a moral framework lest they wander off. We are animals at the end of the day. And like animals we sometimes have to be put in our pen for the greater good. I think restoring a moral absolute is for the greater good for the vast majority of people and society.

The only solution based counterpoint I have heard for this perspective involves empowering the reasoning of the masses so that the mechanism that involves reasoning toward the id, is reasoned toward the superego. I doubt I have to explain why we this would not work. We have invested trillions of dollars to increase the logical reasoning of humans. But in times of pressure, the thinking brain does not win out. It is most often the instinctual mind. Has there ever been a person alive that is instinctually logical?
