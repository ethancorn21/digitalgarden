# productivity to do

Reverse-Calendar Audit: At the end of every hour, log what you actually did to identify the gap between your plan and reality.

Implementation Intentions: Define granular tasks (e.g., specific CSS for a site) instead of generic blocks like "study" to reduce friction.

Energy Mapping: Schedule high-logic tasks like Python or accounting for your peak energy hours and admin tasks for low energy.

Transition Buffers: Use 5-minute resets between context shifts to clear "attention residue" before starting a new task.

Micro-Deadlines: Set a physical timer for slightly less time than you think a task requires to prevent work from expanding.

Focus Mode: Eliminate "small interruptions" by silencing all notifications during deep work blocks to maintain your mental map.

Research Parking Lot: Jot down unrelated technical questions that arise during work to look up later, avoiding rabbit holes.

Definition of Done: Write down exactly what "finished" looks like for a task before starting so you know exactly when to stop.
